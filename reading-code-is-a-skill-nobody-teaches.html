Reading code is a skill nobody teaches

22 Feb 2026 · 6 min · engineering

Most engineers are taught how to write code. Far fewer are taught how to read code that somebody else wrote five years ago for reasons they barely remember.

Reading before editing

One of the most valuable habits I picked up was learning to sit with a codebase long enough for its shape to become legible. That usually means tracing call paths, following data models, and resisting the urge to “clean things up” before I understand why they ended up that way.

Why it matters

In mature systems, most mistakes do not come from typing the wrong thing. They come from changing the wrong thing confidently.

What good readers do

  • notice naming patterns
  • follow invariants
  • look for edges rather than happy paths
  • treat comments as clues, not guarantees

Writing gets most of the prestige. Reading is where a lot of the real leverage lives.
