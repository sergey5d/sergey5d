// projects

Things I've built, broken, and occasionally finished.

A working ledger of side projects, open-source tools, and a few client jobs that survived to production. Status is honest — if it says archived, it's archived.

  • pgshift
    2024 — present

    An ergonomic CLI for zero-downtime Postgres schema changes. Online type conversions, expand–contract migrations, partial backfills with bounded write amplification. Used in production by three companies I know of and probably a few I don't.

    live
    rust postgres cli
  • slowloop
    2025

    A tiny job runner that makes "run this every Tuesday at 3am, but not if it's a holiday in Frankfurt" the boring sentence it should always have been. ~600 lines of Go.

    shipped
    go cron sqlite
  • strange loop 2026 — settlement at 100x
    Mar 2026

    Conference talk on how we rebuilt a payments settlement pipeline to handle a hundredfold increase in volume without rewriting the surrounding 12 services. Slides, recording, and the chart I'm still mildly embarrassed about.

    shipped
    talk distributed-systems
  • tinytrace
    2025

    An opinionated tracing wrapper for small teams who don't want to host an OTel collector. Stores spans in Postgres, ships a small browser UI for searching them. Single static binary. Not the right tool for big shops; perfect for everyone else.

    in progress
    rust otel observability
  • letterhash
    2023

    A weekly newsletter on infrastructure failures, postmortem culture, and the boring parts of reliability engineering. Ran for 18 months and 64 issues before I needed my weekends back. Archive still online.

    archived
    writing
  • cassette.fm
    2022

    A side project that turned into the kind of personal music player I always wanted: BPM-aware shuffle, mood tagging, "what was I listening to in March 2018" search. Self-hosted, single user, intentionally unscalable.

    live
    elixir liveview music
  • paperclip
    2021

    A Chrome extension that quietly archived every PDF I downloaded for tax purposes, then OCR'd them into a searchable corpus. Solved a real problem, then Apple changed something and it stopped working. I never fixed it.

    archived
    typescript ocr
  • this site
    2026

    A small personal site built on the Ledgerline design language, inverted to a darker palette. Hand-written HTML, no framework, no build step. The way websites used to feel.
